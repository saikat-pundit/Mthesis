*   **10Y - 3M Spread:** Currently **+73 bps** (4.57% - 3.84%). The severe inversion that plagued 2023-2024 has normalized. The recession warning light has switched off, replaced by a neutral-to-positive slope.
*   **30Y - 2Y Spread:** Currently **+93 bps** (5.09% - 4.16%). This "Global Liquidity Proxy" is steepening rapidly. A widening spread here typically indicates improving long-term growth expectations or, more cynically, a breakdown in confidence in long-term fiscal discipline.
*   **30Y - 5Y Spread:** Widening significantly, reflecting the market's reluctance to lock in rates at the long end without a substantial premium over the medium term.

**Housing & Unemployment:**
The unemployment rate has ticked up to **4.30% (+20 bps YoY)**, a sign of labor market cooling. Simultaneously, **Housing Supply** has exploded **+14.44% YoY** and **+18.39% QoQ**. This inventory glut is a direct function of the **10Y Yield (4.57%)** and **30Y Yield (5.09%)**. Mortgage rates remain prohibitive, locking sellers in and pricing buyers out, leading to a buildup of unsold inventory.

**Conclusion:**
The consumer is bracing for lower prices (per expectations) but suffering from current costs. The rising unemployment combined with a housing logjam suggests the next six months will be difficult for household balance sheets. The divergence between *feeling* poor (negative real wages) and *expecting* relief (low 1Y inflation) is the fragile hinge upon which the consumer spending story turns. If actual CPI does not converge quickly to the 2.4% expectation, the psychological contract with the consumer will break, triggering a sharper downturn in retail and housing activity.
