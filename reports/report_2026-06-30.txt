### 3. Bonds

*   **Preferred Duration & Credit Quality**
    *   *Duration:* Ultra-short duration (0 to 12 months) and floating-rate instruments.
    *   *Credit Quality:* High-grade Investment Grade (IG) corporate bonds and senior secured bank loans.
    *   *Rationale:* Short-duration assets insulate portfolios from capital losses during a bear-steepening cycle. Floating-rate notes benefit from rising benchmark rates, preserving capital value. High-grade IG corporates offer attractive yields with minimal default risk in a resilient economy.
    *   *Global Money Flows:* Risk-averse institutional capital is moving into short-term US corporate debt and money market funds to preserve liquidity and capture yield, bypassing longer-duration sovereign debt.

*   **Avoid Duration & Credit Quality**
    *   *Duration:* Long duration (15+ years).
    *   *Credit Quality:* Speculative-grade High Yield (HY) credit.
    *   *Rationale:* Long-duration assets suffer severe mark-to-market losses as long-end yields rise. High-yield credit is vulnerable because rising refinancing costs at the long end of the curve pressure weaker corporate balance sheets.
    *   *Global Money Flows:* Capital is actively exiting long-term bond mutual funds and ETFs, accelerating the upward pressure on long-end yields.
